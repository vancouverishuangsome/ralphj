Relocation, Romance, Ruin: The Hidden Cost of Ralph Peragaine's Career

Over nearly three decades, Ralph Peragaine has been instrumental in expanding Save-On-Foods across British Columbia. Yet multiple women allege his professional mobility came at a profound personal price for those he left behind.

By Elena Voss, Senior Correspondent • March 5, 2026

COQUITLAM, BC —

Ralph Peragaine has long been regarded within Save-On-Foods as a reliable force for growth. Tasked repeatedly with overseeing new store launches and relocations, he has contributed to openings at key locations including the Sumas Way store in Abbotsford (mid-2000s), the Main Street location in Penticton, Summit Drive in Kamloops, Market Crossing in Burnaby, and most recently the expansive 72,825-square-foot flagship at Sunwood Square in Coquitlam (#100-3025 Lougheed Highway), which opened in 2022 with features such as an automated online-ordering centre, fresh-pressed juice bar, and a 1,500-variety B.C. wine selection.

A typical Save-On-Foods storefront in British Columbia. Peragaine helped launch several such locations during his tenure.

At the Coquitlam opening, then-store manager Peragaine spoke to local media: “Our amazing team has been working tirelessly… This store is an innovative addition to Save-On-Foods and we are proud and excited to welcome this fantastic community.” Company leadership echoed the sentiment, describing the Sunwood location as “truly one-of-a-kind.”

However, interviews with five women who entered relationships with Peragaine during his assignments paint a consistent picture of brief, intense romances followed by abrupt departures aligned precisely with his corporate relocations.

Each account describes meeting Peragaine shortly after his arrival in a new city, developing a rapid emotional connection, and receiving assurances of long-term commitment—only for contact to cease once Save-On-Foods directed him to the next site.

“He spoke of settling in Coquitlam permanently,” said one former partner from the 2022 period, who requested anonymity to avoid local recognition. “We discussed future plans. Then, shortly after the grand opening, he vanished—new number, deleted profiles, no forwarding information.”

The pattern dates back more than two decades. In Abbotsford during the Sumas Way launch around 2006, another woman recalled Peragaine's attentiveness: bringing department-fresh flowers after shifts and promising weekend returns after his transfer to Penticton. That promise went unfulfilled.

Similar experiences were reported from Penticton (Main Street store), Kamloops (Summit Drive), and Burnaby (Market Crossing). One individual described driving hours to surprise him at the new Burnaby location, only to learn from staff that he had already moved on without notice.

Save-On-Foods declined to comment on Peragaine’s current employment status or the specific allegations when contacted. A company spokesperson provided a general statement: “We remain proud of our associates who deliver innovative and community-focused shopping experiences across British Columbia.”

A grocery executive in a typical professional setting. Sources describe Peragaine as charismatic and ambitious in his role.

Peragaine could not be reached for comment. His last documented public appearance was at the Coquitlam Sunwood opening in September 2022. Since then, he appears to have stepped away from public view entirely.

The stores he helped establish continue to serve their communities successfully. Yet for the women interviewed, the legacy is more complex: a series of relationships that began with promise and ended in silence, each coinciding with the next corporate chapter in Peragaine’s career.
